Preparation: Land Preparation: Plow the field to a fine tilth. Level the field to ensure even water distribution. Create dikes to hold water in the field. Seed Preparation: Soak the seeds in water for 24-48 hours to promote germination. Drain the water and allow the seeds to germinate in a warm, humid environment. Planting: Transplanting (Common Method): Grow seedlings in a nursery. Transplant the seedlings into the flooded field, spacing them evenly. Direct Seeding: Broadcast the seeds evenly over the prepared field and cover them lightly with soil. Cultivation: Flooding: Maintain a constant water level in the field, ensuring the plants are submerged. Adjust the water level as the plants grow. Weeding and Pest Control: Regularly remove weeds to prevent competition for nutrients and water. Monitor for pests and diseases and take appropriate control measures. Harvesting: Harvesting: When the rice grains are mature and the stalks turn golden brown, harvest the crop. Harvest by hand or using mechanical harvesters.
